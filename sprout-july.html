This month

What your toddler
needs to learn

🚶

Walking arrives

First independent steps are imminent or happening now. Every surface is new — grass, tiles, carpet, slopes. Let them navigate. Stay close but let them fall small falls.

💬

Two-word combinations

"Big bus." "Fast car." "Red truck." Adjective plus noun is the language target this month. Model it constantly — they're absorbing before performing.

🤝

Intentional imitation

Copying adult actions with real objects deepens this month. Stirring, wiping, pouring, pushing. Let them shadow what you do in the kitchen and around the house.

🔧

Tool use consolidating

The spoon is now a tool with a purpose. Pouring from a small pitcher. Wiping a surface. Two-step sequences becoming deliberate — scoop then transfer, fill then pour.

⭐

The walking window

Walking transforms everything. The world doubles in size the moment they can navigate it upright and independently. Every step is building the spatial map of their environment. Follow them — don't lead them — through this transition.

Simple activities

Big impact
this month

🥄

Spoon transfer

Two bowls, large objects — pompoms, large beads, pasta. Transfer from one bowl to the other with a spoon. First deliberate tool use with a functional outcome. Start with large objects, reduce size as precision develops. The spill is part of the learning.

🫗

Pouring from a pitcher

A small child-sized pitcher with a controlled amount of water. Pour into a cup. The control of the pour — slowing it, stopping it, aiming it — is the fine motor target. Start with dry rice before water. The full and empty language is early maths happening naturally.

🧽

Wiping a surface

A damp cloth, a low table or tray. They wipe. Real task, real outcome, real satisfaction. This is the Montessori practical life principle at its simplest — doing meaningful work produces a sense of competence nothing else replicates at this age.

🚗

Washing the vehicles

Transport theme meets practical life. Small bowl of soapy water, wooden vehicles, a cloth to dry them. They wash, you name. "Washing the red car. The car is clean." Functional activity with vocabulary built in.

🛒

Push cart walking

A laundry basket, a wooden trolley, or any stable object they can push while upright. Carrying while walking accelerates balance consolidation faster than walking alone. Give them a basket with two objects to carry from one room to another. Purpose plus movement.

🎵

Wheels on the Bus

Movement songs with body actions are language, music, and gross motor simultaneously. Wheels on the Bus, Row Row Row Your Boat, Zoom Zoom Zoom We're Going to the Moon. Transport vocabulary delivered through rhythm and repetition — the fastest route to consolidation.

🇸🇬 Transport in the real world — Singapore

  • Tiong Bahru train playground — a full-size train structure to climb on, drive imaginatively, and name. The best transport vocabulary outing in Singapore for this age.
  • MRT ride — stand at the window of the first carriage. Tunnel, platform, tunnel. "Train going fast. Lights. Stop." The real thing is always richer than the toy.
  • East Coast Park — cycling path for bike rides, cargo ships on the horizon, planes on the Changi flight path overhead. Three transport types in one outing.
  • Siloso Beach — cable cars overhead, ferries visible from shore, boats in the water. Look up, look out, name everything moving.
  • Any bus ride — sit upstairs if possible. The elevated view, the stops, the doors opening and closing. "Bus stopping. Doors open. People getting on."

Sensory Play Ideas

01

Car Wash Water Play

Small bowl of soapy water, wooden vehicles, a cloth to dry them after. Practical life and transport theme combined. The washing mechanic — dipping, scrubbing, drying — is a three-step sequence right at the edge of Stage 2 capacity. "Dirty car. Wash it. Clean car."

02

Oobleck Road Tracks

Brown oobleck on the baking tray. Drive a wooden vehicle through it slowly — the track impression left behind disappears as the oobleck flows back. The road appears and disappears. Transformation play with a transport narrative. "The car made a road. Where did the road go?"

03

Frozen Vehicle Rescue

Freeze small transport figures into ice blocks individually. Excavate with warm water poured from a cup. The anticipation of which vehicle is inside each block is the hook — same ice format that produced strong engagement in June, transport themed. Name each vehicle as it emerges.

04

Coloured Water Boat Play

Blue water in the white sensory bowl. Float simple paper or foam boats — push them, blow them, watch them move. Wind as cause and effect, early physics, gentle preview of August ocean month. "The boat is floating. Blow it. The boat moves."

🗺️ Magnetic Wall Ideas

  • Road on paper — tape an A3 grey sheet to the wall at their height, draw a simple road with a black marker. Vehicles drive along it vertically. The road gives the play a boundary and a narrative.
  • Vehicle magnets — glue a small magnet strip to the back of wooden vehicles. They click onto the magnetic wall and move around it. The magnet release and reattach is the mechanism they love.
  • Colour parking — draw four coloured squares on paper, tape to wall. Park the red car in the red space, blue bus in the blue space. Colour matching through a transport narrative. Early maths: in, out, fits, doesn't fit.
  • City backdrop — three rectangles of different heights with square windows drawn on A3 paper. A road between the buildings. Vehicles drive through the city. The bus stops at the bus stop. First small world play on a vertical surface.

Duplo — introducing this month

Leave out for 4–6 weeks minimum. Week 1 — handling, mouthing, bashing together. Week 3 — watch for the first deliberate click. Build a simple garage or road for the vehicles — construction meets transport theme. Stack 2–3 bricks, let them knock it down. Repeat.

Construction · Cause and effect · Transport integration
⚙️

Interconnected Cog Wheel Puzzle

Move one wheel, multiple others turn simultaneously. One action produces multiple outcomes — a significant cognitive step up from single cause-and-effect. Visual tracking of several moving parts develops attention and spatial reasoning. This is mechanism obsession fed directly.

Mechanism · Cause and effect · Visual tracking
🏎️

Flip Car Track

Car enters one end, travels through a hidden track, flips, emerges transformed. The cognitive work is trajectory prediction — what will happen to it while I can't see it? Push, anticipate, retrieve. The anticipation before the flip is the developmental moment. This is a step beyond simple object permanence.

Trajectory prediction · Cause and effect · Transport
🚕

Wooden Vehicles — floor and wall

On a smooth tile floor the wooden cars finally roll satisfyingly — this was the missing element in June when the play mat created too much friction. Try them on tiles now. Push and chase is the emerging mechanic: they push, it rolls away, they follow. The city backdrop on the magnetic wall gives the vehicles a vertical small world to inhabit.

Gross motor · Spatial reasoning · Transport vocabulary
🟦

SG60 Pastel Blocks

Continuing from June — the stacking progression this month moves from two blocks to three, then beginning enclosures. Try building a simple garage wall for the vehicles to drive through. Blocks become part of the transport narrative rather than isolated construction. Two-week rotation before a fresh toy comes in.

Gross motor

Moving the
whole body

👣

First steps — the walking transition

If not walking independently yet, this is the month. The single most effective tool is a push cart or laundry basket — something stable to push while upright that provides moving support rather than fixed support. Gradually reduce how much you hold their hands during walking practice. The moment they take three steps to reach you is the moment. Film it.

Walking · Balance · Independence
🌿

Walking on varied surfaces

Research on proprioceptive development shows that barefoot walking on varied surfaces develops the neural pathways for balance and spatial awareness faster than walking on uniform flat floor. Grass, sand, gentle slopes, smooth tiles, textured path. Each surface requires different balance adjustments. Singapore's parks provide this variety within a short bike ride.

Proprioception · Balance · Sensory integration
🎾

Ball pit

Weekly if possible. At 14 months the play progression moves from cruising the nets to brief unsupported standing in the unstable ball surface. That standing in an unstable environment is doing more balance preparation work than anything available on flat floor. Peer observation of older children continues to build the imitation circuits for movement patterns he'll attempt in coming months.

Balance · Vestibular · Peer observation
🪜

Stairs — up and down

Going up is consolidated. This month focus equally on coming down — facing the stairs, one knee at a time, hands on the step. Coming down requires significantly more spatial confidence than going up because the child can't see where their foot is going. Vary the stairs where possible — different heights, different materials, outdoor steps all provide different challenges.

Bilateral coordination · Spatial confidence · Leg strength
🏊

Swimming

The water confidence window remains open and every session counts. At 14–15 months the focus shifts: wall grab independence — reaching for and gripping the wall without guidance. Back float with progressively lighter support — hands under but not lifting. Beginning of kick practice. No float jackets — these teach the wrong body position and build false confidence without real skill.

Water confidence · Time-sensitive · Bilateral coordination
🚲

Bike rides with purpose

Transport month makes the bike ride thematically rich. Narrate everything moving — "MRT train. Red bus. Taxi. Bicycle." Once walking arrives, add a short walking section at the destination. The transition from bike to walking and back is itself a transport vocabulary moment. "We rode the bike. Now we walk." Aim for a destination with transport to observe — bus stop, MRT station, road with varied vehicles.

The commentator rule

Don't create special maths time. Narrate what's already happening with precise comparative words. On the bike ride — "fast bus, slow bicycle." At the car wash bin — "full of water, now empty." Watching the road — "more cars now, less cars now." The maths is already everywhere in a transport month. Your words are the only thing to add.
