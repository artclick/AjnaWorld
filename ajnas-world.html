How we play

The principles
behind everything

🖐️

Hands first

Ajna learns through touch before anything else. Let him feel, dig, squeeze and explore before naming. The hands are building the mind.

💬

Name everything

Simple, specific language attached to what he's doing right now. "Big cow." "Full bowl." "In the mud." This is how vocabulary consolidates.

🔁

Repetition is learning

Doing the same thing 20 times isn't boring — it's how his brain builds the connection. Follow his lead when he repeats.

⏱️

Short is enough

5–10 minutes of full engagement beats an hour of half-attention. End while he still wants more. Leave him wanting to come back.

🌿

Free play first

Unstructured self-directed time is the biggest developmental block of every day. Don't fill it. Let him be bored — that's where curiosity lives.

❤️

Your presence

No phone. No multitasking. Your calm, curious attention beside him is the most developmental thing in the room. Everything else is secondary.
